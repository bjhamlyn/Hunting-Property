Frequently Asked Questions

  • Q: What is a non-profit hunting property?

    A: A non-profit hunting property is a hunting location that is owned or leased by a non-profit organization. These organizations typically provide hunting opportunities for a specific group of people, such as veterans or first responders, and may use the proceeds from hunting fees to support their mission.

  • Q: Who can hunt on a non-profit hunting property?

    A: The eligibility requirements for hunting on a non-profit hunting property will vary depending on the organization. Some may require participants to be veterans or first responders, while others may be open to the general public. Be sure to check the organization's website or contact them directly for more information.

  • Q: What types of game are typically available on non-profit hunting properties?

    A: The types of game available will depend on the location and the season. Non-profit hunting properties may offer opportunities to hunt big game, small game, waterfowl, upland birds, or predators. Check the organization's website or contact them directly for more information on the specific game available.

  • Q: Do I need a license to hunt on a non-profit hunting property?

    A: Yes, you will typically need a valid hunting license and any required tags or permits to hunt on a non-profit hunting property. Make sure to check with the organization and the local wildlife agency for any specific requirements.

  • Q: How much does it cost to hunt on a non-profit hunting property?

    A: The cost to hunt on a non-profit hunting property will vary depending on the location and the organization. Some may offer discounted rates for veterans or first responders, while others may have a set fee for all hunters. Check the organization's website or contact them directly for more information on pricing.

  • Q: Are there accommodations available at non-profit hunting properties?

    A: Accommodations will vary depending on the organization and the location. Some non-profit hunting properties may offer lodging or camping facilities, while others may require hunters to make their own arrangements. Check the organization's website or contact them directly for more information on accommodations.

  • Q: Can I bring my own hunting equipment?

    A: Yes, hunters are typically allowed to bring their own hunting equipment to non-profit hunting properties. Make sure to check with the organization for any specific restrictions or requirements.

  • Q: How can I support non-profit hunting properties?

    A: There are several ways to support non-profit hunting properties, including making a donation, volunteering your time, or spreading the word about the organization's mission. Check the organization's website or contact them directly for more information on how you can get involved.
